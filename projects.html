Genetic variation

Genetic variation is the difference in DNA sequences between individuals within a population. There are multiple sources of genetic variation, including mutation and genetic recombination. Genetic variation is important because a population has a better chance of surviving and flourishing than a population with limited genetic variation. Genetic variation can also help us understand how different species are related to each other.
